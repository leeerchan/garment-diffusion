Abstract

Garment sewing patterns serve as the fundamental link between garment design and practical manufacturing. The generative modeling of digital sewing patterns is essential for diverse and creative garment design. However, existing approaches are limited by either a single input modality or suboptimal generation efficiency. In this work, we introduce GarmentDiffusion, a new generative model that is capable of producing centimeter-precise, vectorized 3D sewing patterns from multi-modal inputs. Our method efficiently encodes 3D sewing pattern coordinates into condensed edge token representations, achieving a sequence length 10× shorter than that of the previous auto-regressive model, i.e., DressCode. By employing a diffusion transformer, we concurrently denoise all edge tokens along the temporal axis, maintaining a constant number of denoising steps regardless of dataset-specific edge and panel statistics. We also establish a new SOTA on the largest parametric sewing pattern dataset, GarmentCodeData.

Tokenization




we encode edge-related parameters along the embedding dimension, while denoising all edge tokens in parallel along the temporal axis. After rotating and translating the panels into 3D space, each edge is represented by its start endpoint, control point(s), stitch tag, and stitch flag. The edges form a panel following zero-padding. Each edge is assigned an edge order index and a panel index to indicate its order and its association with the specific panel.



Network architecture




During training, the edge sequence with injected noise is encoded and combined with embeddings of edge order index, panel index, and timestep before being fed into the transformer network for prediction. After embedding the text and image conditions, decoupled cross-attention is used to compute the attention outputs separately for each condition while sharing the query. The results are then summed to achieve joint control of both text and image conditions. Automatic pattern completion is achieved by noise replacement with user-provided panel during inference.



Data annotation pipeline




We created text and sketch modalities for the DressCode and GarmentCode (v2) datasets.
